CODING

For as long as I can remember, I've been intrigued by the notion of learning to code. I'd consider taking a course, but life would be busy and I'd accept that "I don't have time right now."

Towards the tail end of 2020, I made the decision to just go for it. Start somewhere and see where it takes me. After researching the endless list of options available online, I registered for my first course:

RESPONSIVE WEB DESIGN

In this Responsive Web Design Certificate program, I learned the languages that developers use to build webpages: HTML for content, and CSS for design.

After mastering the basics, I moved on to more modern techniques like CSS variables, along with best practices for accessibility. Finally, I learned how to make webpages that respond to different screen sizes with CSS Grid.

View Certificate

After earning freeCodeCamp's Responsive Web Design certificate, I jumped right into their next certificate program: JavaScript Algorithms and Data Structures. This was my first experience with a more complex language, requiring algorithmic computational thinking. While I was loving what I was learning, I ultimately decided to take a step back and ground my educational journey in a more traditional introduction to computer science course.

With so many options available, I initially suspected that it would be a challenge to choose one. However, the overwhelming majority of opinions I heard and read pointed me in the direction of the undisputed world champion of CS courses:

CS50X: INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTER SCIENCE

Harvard University's introduction to the intellectual enterprises of computer science and the art of programming for majors and non-majors alike, with or without prior programming experience.

This course taught me how to think algorithmically and solve problems efficiently. The topics covered include abstraction, algorithms, data structures, encapsulation, resource management, security, software engineering, and web development.

The programming languages covered include C, Python, SQL, and JavaScript plus CSS and HTML. Throughout the curriculum, I completed problem sets inspired by real-world domains of biology, cryptography, finance, forensics, and gaming.

View Course Info

CS50x has not only truly helped me to jumpstart my educational path, it has also confirmed for me that I'm running in the right direction. It's been a struggle at times, but I know that's completely normal and expected for anyone starting out in this space.

It's those moments when frustration is at a 10, but then I figure out the solution, that I feel on top of the world. Even while I was in the midst of taking the course, I wanted more and more. So, I decided to take on another challenge simultaneously and dive into an even deeper end of the dev pool...

INTRODUCTION TO MACHINE LEARNING

This course provided me with a foundational understanding of machine learning models (e.g. logistic regression, multilayer perceptrons, convolutional neural networks, natural language processing, etc.) as well as how these models can solve complex problems in a variety of industries, from medical diagnostics to image recognition to text prediction.

The practice exercises gave me hands-on experience implementing these data science models on data sets, and taught me how to implement machine learning algorithms with PyTorch.

View Certificate

Completing this course confirmed my fascination of the machine learning space. I plan to continue digging deeper into this space, as I consider it to be one I can envision spending my career with.

In the short-term, I am focused on completing the last 2 weeks of CS50x and the final project, along with completing freeCodeCamp's JS Algorithms and Data Structures certificate. I recently revisited that course, now with the knowledge and experience of CS50x in my favor. It's been a major confidence booster to see how much more intuitive the course materials now feel.

I'm truly relishing my time soaking up all this knowledge and I'm beyond excited for what's to come in my future!
