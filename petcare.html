PET CARE

Growing up, I desperately wanted a dog. In a house filled with allergy-ridden family members, though, I knew early on that it was never going to happen. I vowed that when I grew up and lived on my own, I would have as many pets as I wanted!

My parents still did their best to bring pets into our family, given the restrictions. Over the years, we had dozens of fish, some hermit crabs and even two hooded rats (not at the same time, of course). There was even a winter break during elementary school, in which our classroom hamster came to stay in my house while school was closed.

I got my fill of "real pets" when I would visit friends and family, but it was never enough.

In college, I knew I wasn't ready to take on the responsibility of a dog fulltime, but that didn't stop me from taking in a friend's Beagle puppy for a couple of weeks after her landlord evicted the pup. That was only temporary until she found a new home for him, although I hoped that it would take a long time. It didn't.

Not too long after college, I met my wife, Jessica. To my delight, she grew up with REAL pets her entire life, and at the time had 2 dogs and a cat. There are countless reasons why I fell in love with Jessica, but the animals definitely helped!

While we were dating, Jessica adopted two more cats (Ava + Tila). When Bailey, one of her family's Irish Setters, became a Grand Champion in the AKC, he became a desirable "stud", if you know what I mean! Before long, Bailey was a father and one of the pups from the litter (Sonny) came to live with Jessica's family.

Most weekends, I would visit Jess, her family and all the fur kids. She would also bring Ava + Tila to come visit me. It wasn't the same as having my own pets, but it was about as close as I could get without taking that plunge.

After Jess and I got engaged, we moved in together. Not only did I get to now live with the love of my life, but Ava + Tila joined us! I was officially a pet dad! Less than a year later, Sonny followed his cat sisters and came to live with us as well. What a dream!

It's been more than 10 years now, and Sonny and Tila are still filling our lives with joy on a daily basis. Our sweet girl Ava crossed the rainbow bridge at the beginning of 2021. We miss so much about her, but are forever grateful for the time we had with her.

×

For someone who grew up without pets, but always wanted them, you'd think I'd be satisfied with my adulthood fur family. Well, not only did we have our pets, but we decided to make our passion into our profession.

It started first with Jessica, but after founding and growing a pet care company on her own for 2.5 years, I left my career in advertising and joined her over five years ago. Through Gold Coast Pet Care, our extended fur family is now in the hundreds!

I guess I kept my promise to my childhood self!
